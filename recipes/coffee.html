Coffee

Description

Coffee is a brewed drink prepared from roasted coffee beans,
the seeds of berries from certain flowering plants in the Coffea genus.
From the coffee fruit, the seeds are separated to produce a stable,
raw product: unroasted green coffee.
The seeds are then roasted, a process which transforms them into a consumable product: roasted coffee,
which is ground into fine particles that are typically steeped in hot water before being filtered out, producing a cup of coffee.

Ingredients

  • ½ pound coarsely ground coffee beans
  • 4 ½ cups cold water
  • cheesecloth
  • coffee filters

Steps

  1. Put coffee grounds in a large container. Slowly pour water over the grounds.
  2. Cover bowl with plastic wrap and steep at room temperature for 18 to 24 hours.
  3. Line a strainer with several layers of cheesecloth and place atop a pitcher;
    strain coffee through the cheesecloth into the pitcher.
    Discard the grounds. To get a clearer brew, strain coffee again through coffee filters. Store in refrigerator.
